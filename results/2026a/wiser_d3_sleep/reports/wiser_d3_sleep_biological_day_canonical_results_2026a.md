## Evidence status (two levels)

**Supported within the current WISER measurement (descriptive):**
- Detected within-trunk site changes are **not** concentrated near 10:00 (A1: 11 % within ±1 h; A2: 8 %).
- The two house-labelled ROIs account for **~85 %** of classified low-movement trunk dwell (unconditional).
- The state-sequence detects **multiple** qualifying transitions per rat-day (mean 3.1); ~half of interpretable relocations touch a non-house state.
- WISER locomotor emergence generally occurs **near the evening boundary** (~20.8 h), with no detectable monotonic temperature association over 11 days.
- Stable **individual differences** in primary house and relocation rate.

**Candidate biological interpretation (not established):**
- The low-movement trunk corresponds to physiological sleep.
- Classified ROIs correspond exactly to specific physical refuge structures (frame unverified).
- Doorway/near-water use on hot days reflects thermoregulation.
- Evening emergence clustering is generated by a circadian mechanism.
- `water_2` represents use of the water-tower refuge.

## Interpretation limits (apply to all of the above)
- Sleep = low-speed **proxy** (no ephys/interior-CV validation).
- WISER inch frame **unverified** → no directional/absolute-location or microclimate claims.
- `refuge_4` (burrow) and `tunnel` are **interpretation-limited**; excluded from the 110 interpretable relocations and flagged in dwell.
- `doorway` / `exposed` are classifier-dependent and jitter-adjacent.
- Ambient (not in-shelter) temperature; 11 temperature days; uncorrected multiple comparisons; repeated observations of the same 5 rats with shared day-level weather (not 55 independent exposures).
